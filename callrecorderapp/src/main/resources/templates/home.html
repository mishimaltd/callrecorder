Frequently Asked Questions

How does your service work?

We provide a simple automated call recording service that you can use to record any outgoing phone call in high-quality audio. To use our service you just need to:

  1. Call our toll free automated number
  2. You will be asked to enter the number of the person you want to connect to on your telephone keypad followed by the # key.
  3. The service will read back that number to you and ask you to press '1' on your telephone keypad to confirm.
  4. The service will then connect you to that number. The person being called will see your number on the incoming call, not ours.
  5. At the end of your call just hang up and we'll send you an email with a secure link to your recording.
  6. You can access any of your call recordings at any time by going to your private account dashboard.

Can I try it out before registering?

Absolutely! You can try out our service for free by calling our toll-free trial number. At the end of your call we'll send you a text message with a link to your securely-stored recording.

Our trial service is limited to a maximum call duration of five minutes, your call will automatically disconnect after five minutes has elapsed.

How do I get access to my recording?

At the end of your call we'll automatically send you an email with a link to your securely-stored recording.

You can also access any of your saved recordings at any time by going to your secure account dashboard.

How does the billing and payment work?

You will be charged 15¢ per minute for your call recording. Billing starts from the moment the call recipient picks up the phone and stops when you end the call.

Payment on your account is settled on a monthly basis based on your usage. You only pay for what you use at 15¢ per minute. If you do not use the servive, you pay nothing.

Our service uses a toll free number so your telephone provider will not charge you at all for using our service.

What happens if nobody picks up?

We only start charging you from the moment your call is connected to your intended recipient at 15¢ per minute. If nobody picks up the call then you will not be charged.

Is it legal to record a telephone call?

Federal law permits recording telephone conversations and in-person conversations so long as you are a party to that conversation. This means that it is legal for you to record a phone call that you either initiate or receive.

Certain states have adopted what is referred to as 'two-party consent' which means that it is your legal responsibility to ensure that all parties to the conversation give their consent to it being recorded.

Two party consent laws are applicable if either you or the other party is resident in the following states: California, Connecticut, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Montana, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania and Washington.

If in doubt you should always ask the other party for their consent before recording a call.
